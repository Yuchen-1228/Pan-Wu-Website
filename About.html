Mission Statement




Let our tears and sweats be the proof of our hard work,
and let our dreams and goals be the reality of our future!
It’s a difficult time transitioning from remote back to in-person learning. Cramming homework
and projects at 3 A.M. and studying for tests the night before, our workload never seems to end.


Those moments definitely sounded familiar. When everyone is pressured by the Stuyvesant
atmosphere, they feel lost. What do we want to do? What do we want to accomplish? These are
all questions to think about while facing our Stuyvesant stress. We want people to find their
purpose, to think about what they want to become in the future, and to search for their own paths.

As rising juniors, we’ll be stressing over SAT, ACT, Common APP preparations, and more APs.
It will be a stressful year. And it’ll mark the climax of our high school journey. It’s crucial for us
to start planning our future.


Currently, our sophomores are still wandering, struggling, and balancing. Awaiting them in our
vision are academic improvements, balanced student life, extracurricular opportunities, career
and future exploration, and the college navigations.


Our focus is to help our students utilize the most of the available resources. Our caucus
will provide our rising juniors a platform for guidance and support. We’ll help them stay on top of
their college preparation process, securing opportunities, as well as maintaining their deserved youth.


We will connect our students with the school administration and our community, and to create an
engageful environment where everyone can succeed. We want to make the junior year the most
brilliant without regrets to our sophomores and everyone in our Stuyvesant community.


Let our tears and sweats be the proof of our hard work,
and let our dreams and goals be the reality of our future!
